Zero-trust

Zero-trust by construction. Your backups are encrypted before they ever leave your system, so not even the storage provider can read your data. And every snapshot is complete and independent: delete old snapshots without breaking newer ones, or restore from any point in time.

Strategic autonomy

Strategic autonomy in the software supply chain. Open under a permissive license, self-hostable everywhere, with a control plane you can run on-prem, in a regional cloud, or via a managed-service provider. No appliance lock-in, no proprietary data format, no vendor-mediated recovery path. Your data, your keys, your code path.

Cloud-native

Cloud-native and Kubernetes-orchestrated. Plakar's operator turns Kubernetes into the control plane for the resilience layer of your entire estate, not just your clusters. Define backup, retention, replication, and recovery policies as Kubernetes CRDs, Terraform resources, or Ansible playbooks. Version them in Git, reconcile state continuously.

Kubernetes-orchestrated 
kind: BackupPolicy
spec:
  target: postgres-prod
  retention: 30d-immutable
  replication: [ovh, scaleway]
Reconciled by the operator · GitOps via ArgoCD / Flux
Programmable primitive

A programmable primitive for humans, applications, and AI agents. Snapshots are a primary surface, not an exhaust pipe. Because every policy is declarative and every snapshot is governed, AI agents can manage the resilience posture: create policies, query coverage, trigger restores into ephemeral environments, gate model training on verified data, with the same primitives a human operator uses.

★ Your Own Storage

Storage Provider Agnostic

Snapshots land on storage you own, always with zero plaintext: any S3-compatible backend, local disk, or filesystem. On S3-compatible backends, object-lock immutability and append-only keep them tamper-proof.

AWS S3Azure BlobGoogle CloudMinIOBackblaze B2Cloudflare R2NetAppLocal disk

Cloud Native Architecture

Open Resilience as Code.

Resilience as code means your backup posture is defined, versioned, and automated, not clicked through a console. Manage every policy through the Plakar API, a Terraform provider, or Ansible playbooks: schedules, retention, replication, and verification live in Git, reconcile continuously, and ship the same way the rest of your infrastructure does.

plakar — zsh
❯ terraform apply ✓ plakar_backup_policy.postgres-prod created schedule = "*/15 * * * *" · retention = "30d-immutable" replication = [ovh, scaleway] ❯ kubectl apply -f resilience-policy.yaml ✓ BackupPolicy/postgres-prod reconciled · GitOps-ready ❯ ansible-playbook resilience.yml ✓ 12 hosts · policies converged ❯ curl -s api.plakar.io/v1/coverage ✓ {"protected": 1420, "verified": "100%"}
